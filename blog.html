Parachute Testing Testing

We dropped the prototype can on 2 separate occasions, one with the 9V battery and one without it. Both were from a 9.1m high position onto a hard wooden surface. No sufficient/major damage was experienced by the can during the first drop with its weight at only around 150g. During the second weighted drop, with the can weighing about 200g, it experienced a fracture in the top part of the can and part of the friction fit for the battery was broken although the battery itself did not fall out of its designated space. Using tracking software and some basic calculations, we discovered that the final impact velocity for the second drop was 7.25m/s, which, when held in consideration with the fact that it was a hard surface, (unlike our actual drop) suggests that foam padding will not be needed. Can Stress Testing Testing

Applied a mass of 22.6kg to the can whilst it was stationary. This is equal to 221.7N of compressive force. Using Force = Change in KE/ distance, assuming the distance to be around 20cm of long grass, we have Force = (0.5 x 0.3 x 10 x 10) / 0.2 = 75N. This shows that our can’s compressive strength is definitely enough to survive the impact with minimal damage.

How Carbon Dioxide affects our planet Environment

Carbon dioxide (CO₂) is a greenhouse gas that significantly impacts the environment, primarily through its role in climate change. By trapping heat in the atmosphere, increased CO₂ levels from human activities such as burning fossil fuels and deforestation contribute to global warming, leading to rising temperatures, extreme weather events, melting glaciers, and rising sea levels. It also dissolves in oceans, causing acidification that harms marine life, particularly corals and shell-building organisms. These changes disrupt ecosystems, forcing species to migrate or adapt, and contribute to biodiversity loss. Agriculture and food security are affected as shifting climate patterns and extreme weather reduce crop yields, while human health faces risks from heatwaves, respiratory issues, and the spread of diseases. Additionally, CO₂-driven warming triggers feedback loops, such as methane release from thawing permafrost, further accelerating climate change. Addressing CO₂ emissions is essential to mitigate these cascading environmental and societal impacts.
